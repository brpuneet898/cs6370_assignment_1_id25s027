Scenario where the proposed top-down tokenizer performs better:
When only simple alphabetic word extraction is desired and punctuation should be discarded completely.
Example: 'engine-performance, fuel-flow, and pressure?'
Naive tokenizer output may be cleaner for a basic IR pipeline: ['engine', 'performance', 'fuel', 'flow', 'and', 'pressure']
PTB and spaCy typically preserve punctuation or split hyphenated forms into more pieces, which may be less convenient in a simplistic indexing setup.

Scenario where the proposed top-down tokenizer performs worse:
When the text contains contractions, abbreviations, decimals, or hyphenated expressions.
Example: "The U.S. aircraft can't maintain 3.5-mile stability."
Naive tokenizer may produce: ['The', 'U', 'S', 'aircraft', 'can', 't', 'maintain', '3', '5', 'mile', 'stability']
This loses important information. PTB and spaCy handle such cases more linguistically.
